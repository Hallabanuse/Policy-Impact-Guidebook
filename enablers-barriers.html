Strategic Implications

  • Amplify the economic case: Treatment costs (€18B+) far exceed transition costs. Frame regulation as fiscally responsible - make officials fear the cost of inaction more than industry pressure.
  • Reduce fear of job losses: Emphasise that "essential uses" remain protected. Officials need political cover - show that medical devices, safety equipment etc. are explicitly exempted.
  • Counter the "blanket ban" narrative: Consistent messaging about "non-essential use regulation" removes the emotional barrier of appearing anti-business.
  • Provide technical support: Help address capacity gaps by providing independent analysis of "essential use" claims - reduce reliance on industry-provided information.
  • Leverage personal relevance: Officials drink the same water - make the health evidence personally salient, not abstract.
  • Make support the easy path: The regional process is already underway - frame supporting it as the low-risk default, opposing it as the politically costly exception.

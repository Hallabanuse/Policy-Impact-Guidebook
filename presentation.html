Chapter 2

A Critical Distinction

Getting this right helps identify where policy can make a difference

Direct Threat

Human ACTION that causes harm

Example: "Industrial PFAS discharge"

You can regulate this

Biophysical Factor

Ecosystem EFFECT of that action

Example: "Water contamination"

This is the result, not the target

Why This Matters

Policy interventions target human actions. If you confuse effects with actions, you may target the wrong thing.

Chapter 2

Policy Context Assessment

Use the Multiple Streams Framework to find policy windows

Problems Stream

Is the issue on the agenda? Focusing events? Indicators? Media attention?

Politics Stream

Public opinion? Interest groups? Government position? Political mood?

Policies Stream

Are solutions available? Technically feasible? Politically acceptable?

When streams converge, a policy window opens.

PFAS Case Example

Problems stream was strong (90%) with clear contamination evidence. A political window was opening due to upcoming elections.

Critical Question

What's the REAL strategic challenge? Frame it correctly. "Advocating for regulation" is different from "defending regulation that's already underway."

PFAS Case Example

The EU restriction was already happening. The real challenge was defending its strength against industry lobbying for exemptions.

Theory of Change - Generic Template

A Theory of Change (or Results Chain) shows the causal assumptions about how your actions will achieve intermediate results and longer-term goals. It transforms the "current state" (situation model) into a "desired state" by mapping the pathway from actions to outcomes.

From Situation Model to Results Chain

Your situation model shows the current state - threats, contributing factors, and their impacts. The Theory of Change flips this: instead of showing what causes harm, it shows what needs to change and how your actions will achieve that change.

Key: Results Chain Elements
Action Set of activities with common focus
Activity Set of tasks within an action
Intermediate Result Result en route to final goal
Threat Reduction Result Reducing a direct threat
Value Ecosystems, species, or wellbeing
Objective Formal statement of desired result
Goal Desired future status of a value
Indicator Measures progress on objective/goal

Key Terms

Action A set of activities with a common focus that work together to achieve specific goals and objectives by targeting key intervention points, optimising opportunities, and limiting constraints
Activity A set of tasks within an overall action
Intermediate Result A specific result that a project is working to achieve en route to accomplishing a final goal or objective
Threat Reduction Result A specific result that a project is working to achieve in terms of reducing a direct threat
Value The ecosystems, species and human well-being values on which the project or program is focused
Goal A formal statement detailing a project's desired impact, especially the desired future status of a value
Objective A formal statement detailing a desired intermediate result of a project, such as reducing a critical threat
Indicator A measurable entity related to a specific information need (e.g., the status of a value, progress toward an objective or goal)
